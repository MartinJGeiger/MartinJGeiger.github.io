Research interests

My research interests revolve around difficult (often: NP-hard) optimization and decision making problems. More precisely, I investigate planning problems, or (more formally): my research is concerned with the assignment of values to variables. Problems of interest can be categorized into two sub-classes:
  1. Combinatorial optimization, with applications in routing, scheduling, timetabling, etc. Here, the objective is to find (optimal) values for a vector of decision variables X, such that some function f(X) assumes a minimal value.
    Employed methods stem from mathematical optimization, graph theory, operations research, and related disciplines.
  2. (Multi-criteria) decision making under uncertainty, with applications in dynamic capital allocation. There, the objective is to find values for a vector of decision variables X, such that some future outcome F(X) = (f1(X), ..., fk(X)) is optimized.
    Relevant methods require a thorough understanding of risk management and the underlying economics of the problem at hand.
